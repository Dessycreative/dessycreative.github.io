Finance Model::01

Three-Statement Financial Model — PnL, Balance Sheet & Cash Flow

Full three-statement model in Power BI. PnL tracks revenue, EBITDA (Net Trading Profit + Depreciation) and cost categories through a Chart of Accounts structure. The Cash Flow page generates AI narrative summaries as Card visuals — DAX-concatenated strings that auto-update on every filter interaction. Balance sheet drill-through exposes all subcodes behind every consolidated line item.

Power BIDAXChart of AccountsEBITDACash FlowDrill-throughStar Schema
// Auto-narrative Cash Flow card Cash Flow Summary = VAR _EBITDA = /* from Chart of Accounts */ VAR _CapEx = /* capital expenditure group */ VAR _Net = Closing Balance - Opening Balance RETURN "EBITDA: " & FORMAT(_EBITDA, "#,##0") & UNICHAR(10) & "CapEx: " & FORMAT(ABS(_CapEx), "#,##0") & UNICHAR(10) & "Net Cash: " & FORMAT(_Net, "#,##0")

Forecasting Model::04

Context-Aware Sales Forecasting Model

Built entirely in Power BI, this forecasting model predicts future sales based on a rolling 6-week actuals window. Bank holidays, seasonal events, and special occasions are factored into the forecast logic — so the model doesn't blindly project off a week where trading was artificially suppressed or inflated. Outputs a forward-looking sales view directly inside the Power BI report, accessible to planners and commercial teams without needing a data science background.

Power BIDAXRolling 6-Week WindowBank Holiday LogicSeasonal AdjustmentSales Forecast
FORECAST LOGIC
● Rolling 6-week actuals as base signal
● Bank holiday flag — excludes or adjusts affected weeks
● Special occasions — Christmas, Easter, events
● Weighted recency — recent weeks carry more influence
● Output: forward-looking weekly sales projection
● Fully interactive — slicers adjust the forecast view
✓ No Python or external tools — pure Power BI
✓ Business users can interact with it directly
✓ Context-aware — not a naive rolling average
